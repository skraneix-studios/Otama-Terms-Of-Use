4. How Otama's App-Blocking Behavior Works — Please Read Carefully

This section is the most important part of these Terms. Please read it before configuring any locks.

4.1 The App is designed to be intentionally hard to bypass.

Otama's purpose is to help you create friction between yourself and apps you want to use less. That friction works only if it is difficult to disable. By design, the App makes it deliberately inconvenient to remove restrictions you have set on yourself. This is the intended behavior, not a malfunction.

Specifically:

  • Interrupt locks can require you to solve puzzles (math problems, memory games, pattern challenges, etc.) before an app you've restricted will open.
  • Cooldowns prevent locks from being instantly bypassed by re-opening an app.
  • Time locks, daily allowances, and saved allowance can limit access based on the choices and quotas you configure.
  • Routines can group multiple apps under a single locking rule.
  • Focus sessions can shield selected apps for a configured period.
  • Quick Lock on Android can hide an app from Otama's launcher.
  • Committed Mode (see Section 5) makes it harder to disable the App's blocking entirely.
  • Device protections, where supported and enabled by you, can prevent app removal or manual time changes.

These mechanisms are working as designed when they prevent you from opening an app, even when you really want to.

4.2 You may be unable to open an app when you need to.

There will be times when Otama prevents you from opening an app and you genuinely want or need to use it — to reply to a message, check something urgently, or because you changed your mind about the restriction you set earlier.

This is the intended behavior of the App. It is the same friction that makes Otama useful at other times. You acknowledge and accept this when you configure any lock.

4.3 If you cannot bypass a lock, the App is doing its job.

If a puzzle is too hard, a cooldown is too long, a time window is too strict, or a Committed Mode challenge is too difficult — the App is working as intended. These restrictions exist because you, the user, configured them. They are not bugs.

  • iOS and iPadOS: Otama's shields use Apple's Screen Time frameworks. You may be able to revoke Otama's Screen Time authorization in system settings, disable the relevant protection inside Otama, or remove the App using Apple's normal app-removal controls.

Before enabling strict protections, make sure you understand the recovery path on your device. 5. Committed Mode

Committed Mode is an optional setting that makes it harder to disable the App's blocking. When you enable Committed Mode:

  • Toggling off protected locks, routines, or sessions may require completing one or more challenges.
  • Switching back from Committed Mode to Casual Mode may require completing a multi-step challenge sequence.
  • Editing certain lock settings may require an untoggle gate or may take effect only after a waiting period, such as at midnight.

You enable Committed Mode voluntarily. It exists specifically to prevent impulse-disabling. If you find yourself unable to complete the required challenges, that is the friction working as designed. Review the platform-specific recovery information in Section 4.4 before enabling it.

We strongly recommend you do not enable Committed Mode unless you understand and accept that you may not always be able to disable it quickly.
