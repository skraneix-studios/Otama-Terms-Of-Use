1. What Otama Does

Otama is an Android focus and screen-time management app. Its core features include:

  • Custom launcher that replaces your default home screen with a minimal, intentional interface.
  • App locks and interrupts that interrupt or block access to other apps on your device based on rules you configure (puzzles, timers, cooldowns, daily limits, and scheduled lock windows).

Otama is a tool for self-regulation. It works only with your active participation — you choose which apps to restrict, what kind of challenges to require, and how strict to make the rules. The App does not block apps you have not configured it to block.

4. How Otama's App-Blocking Behavior Works — Please Read Carefully

This section is the most important part of these Terms. Please read it before configuring any locks.

4.1 The App is designed to be intentionally hard to bypass.

Otama's purpose is to help you create friction between yourself and apps you want to use less. That friction works only if it is difficult to disable. By design, the App makes it deliberately inconvenient to remove restrictions you have set on yourself. This is the intended behavior, not a malfunction.

Specifically:

  • Interrupt locks can require you to solve puzzles (math problems, memory games, pattern challenges, etc.) before an app you've restricted will open.
  • Cooldowns prevent locks from being instantly bypassed by re-opening an app.
  • Time locks and daily limits prevent access to apps during windows you've configured, including overnight and during work hours.
  • Routines can group multiple apps under a single locking rule.
  • Quick Lock can hide an app from your launcher.
  • Committed Mode (see Section 5) makes it harder to disable the App's blocking entirely.

These mechanisms are working as designed when they prevent you from opening an app, even when you really want to.

4.2 You may be unable to open an app when you need to.

There will be times when Otama prevents you from opening an app and you genuinely want or need to use it — to reply to a message, check something urgently, or because you changed your mind about the restriction you set earlier.

This is the intended behavior of the App. It is the same friction that makes Otama useful at other times. You acknowledge and accept this when you configure any lock.

4.3 If you cannot bypass a lock, the App is doing its job.

If a puzzle is too hard, a cooldown is too long, a time window is too strict, or a Committed Mode challenge is too difficult — the App is working as intended. These restrictions exist because you, the user, configured them. They are not bugs.

4.4 The "always-available" escape hatch: uninstall.

If, at any time, you cannot bypass a restriction and need immediate access to your device or to a blocked app, you can uninstall Otama through the Google Play Store or your device's Settings → Apps → Otama → Uninstall. Uninstalling the App removes all of its restrictions immediately. No challenge, puzzle, or cooldown can prevent you from uninstalling the App.

Uninstalling does not require any cooperation from us, and we cannot remotely lock you out of doing it. This option remains available to you at all times, even when you are inside Committed Mode and even when locks are active.

5. Committed Mode

Committed Mode is an optional setting that makes it harder to disable the App's blocking. When you enable Committed Mode:

  • Toggling off the main blocking switch requires completing challenges (typically a pattern challenge, a chimp test, and a slide puzzle).
  • Switching back from Committed Mode to Casual Mode requires completing the same kind of challenge.

You enable Committed Mode voluntarily. It exists specifically to prevent impulse-disabling. If you find yourself unable to complete the required challenges, that is the friction working as designed. As described in Section 4.4, you can always uninstall the App through the Play Store to remove all restrictions immediately, including Committed Mode.

We strongly recommend you do not enable Committed Mode unless you understand and accept that you may not always be able to disable it quickly.
